# Go-to-market

**Exact target user:** VP Engineering or Head of Platform at a **15–80 person Series A** software company in North America who owns the “AI tools” line item on the corporate card but does not live in pricing spreadsheets daily. They approve Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT Business, and API keys, and they feel vague dread every renewal cycle because usage is opaque.

**What they Google or scroll right before they want this:** “ChatGPT Business vs Enterprise pricing,” “GitHub Copilot Business cost per seat,” “Cursor Teams SSO pricing,” “OpenAI invoice line items,” and “Ramp recurring software spend spikes.” They are not searching “AI spend audit” yet—that phrase has to be invented in the creative.

**Where they hang out:** `r/startups` and `r/SaaS` threads about burn, **Lenny’s Newsletter** comments, **Software Engineering Daily** Slack (and similar eng Slack bridges), **Plaid** / **Linear** adjacent Discord communities where eng leaders compare stacks, **X** lists like “infra eng” and “AI eng hiring,” and **Indie Hackers** for the sub-20 person long tail that still buys too many tools.

**First 100 users in 30 days with $0 paid ads**

1. **20 warm audits:** DM twenty Series A eng leaders on X with a personalized one-liner referencing their public job posts (“noticed you’re hiring platform eng—quick stack question”) and offer to run the audit live on a 10-minute call while screen sharing. Close with “can I send the share link for your CFO?”  
2. **15 finance-brokered intros:** Ask three fractional CFOs who serve startups for a blurb they can forward to clients; give them a canned paragraph + link. CFOs love looking smart before board meetings.  
3. **10 design partners from HN “Show HN”:** Post on a Tuesday morning with the live URL, lead with the shareable URL mechanic, and commit to respond to every pricing critique in-thread with citations to `PRICING_DATA.md`.  
4. **25 niche newsletters:** Pitch **TLDR DevOps**, **Console.dev**, and two paid niche newsletters that accept non-sponsored “tooling” blurbs if the angle is “spreadsheet-free vendor math.”  
5. **20 vertical micro-communities:** Post in **Women Who Code** leadership Slack, **Blacks in Technology**, and **LatAm Dev** Discords with a “free stack check” offer—avoid spam by asking mods first.  
6. **10 office hours:** Host a weekly “AI bill office hours” Zoom (no pitch deck, just screenshare audits); record snippets with permission.

**Unfair distribution channel only Credex (or a credit broker) has:** outbound using **real anonymized savings deltas** from inventory conversations—“we just saw a $4k/mo OpenAI commit re-trade at X% off” is not something a random SaaS blog can cite. Credex can pair audits with **live credit quotes** without naming customers, which turns a calculator into a two-sided market teaser.

**Week-1 traction if it works:** 800–1,500 unique visitors, 120–200 completed audits, 35–55 share links created, 20–35 consultation requests from the >$500/mo cohort, 3–5 credit conversations that reach term sheet stage internally.

**Operational detail most teams skip:** prepare a **Notion template** with screenshots of each vendor’s billing console so the person running the audit can paste actual invoice lines in <60 seconds. The calculator is only as good as the spend inputs; removing friction there beats adding another “AI insight” badge.
